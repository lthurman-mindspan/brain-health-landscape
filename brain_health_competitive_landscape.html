New Entrant
NewDays AI is the most notable new entrant since the last update. Seattle-based, $7M seed from General Catalyst and Madrona (Sept 2025), founded by Babak Parviz (former Amazon VP, Google Glass creator) and Daniel Kelly (engineering, Amazon and Google X). Holds exclusive license to the Harvard I-CONECT protocol — a randomized trial showing structured conversational engagement can delay cognitive decline by 6 months to 2.5 years. Live in California (Mindspan's footprint), Washington, Florida, and Texas. The model is distinctive: an AI companion called "Sunny" delivers daily structured exercises between clinician visits, scaling a treatment protocol that was previously bottlenecked by clinician time. Not a direct head-to-head with Mindspan today (no biomarkers, no neurologists, no FDA-therapy infusion pathway), but worth tracking as the AI-companion-driven cognitive care category emerges — and as a possible complement Mindspan could license or partner with for between-visit patient engagement.
II.
DTC Prevention with an Insurance Pathway
A small set — most prevention plays are self-pay
Context
True DTC dementia prevention covered by insurance is rare. Insurance reimburses treatment of diagnosed conditions, not prevention for healthy people. Each company below has found a workaround — billing Category III coaching codes against commercial plans, billing Chronic Care Management against a qualifying diagnosis, contracting with Medicare Advantage plans as an ancillary benefit, or running a hybrid where the clinical front door is covered and the prevention layer is self-pay.

III.
CPT Code Viability for the Mindspan Build
Stacked billing strategy. Hover any code for detail.
The 0591T Question
0591T is viable as a billable code, not as a primary revenue driver. It's Category III (emerging, temporary), reimbursement is at each payer's discretion, and the coach must hold NBHWC or NCHEC certification plus an NPI. CMS added 0591T–0593T to the Telehealth Services List in 2024, but adding to the list does not equal payment. BetterBrain has built their model around this code precisely because they target commercial employer plans — and that is also why their model has a ceiling on the Medicare-age population where dementia risk actually lives.
Tier 1 · Foundation Category I · Reliable
Diagnosed / At-Risk Patients
Reliable revenue base for any prevention program inside a clinical practice. Medicare-covered, broadly accepted by commercial payers.
  • 99483 Cognitive assessment & care plan (~$280, once per year per patient)
  • G0438 G0439 AWV with cognitive screen
  • 99490 CCM, first 20 min/mo ($66 avg)
  • 99439 CCM add-on, additional 20 min ($50/mo, up to 2x)
  • 99487 99489 Complex CCM for higher-acuity patients
Tier 2 · Prevention Layer Category I · Broadly Covered
Undiagnosed / Worried-Well
Lower per-encounter payment, but reaches patients who don't qualify for CCM. Combine with self-pay membership for full revenue picture.
  • 99401–99404 Preventive medicine counseling (15–60 min)
  • 99411–99412 Preventive counseling, group
  • G0444 Annual depression screening (Medicare)
  • 96127 Brief behavioral assessment ($4–6, stackable)
Tier 3 · Coaching Category III · Variable
The 0591T Layer
Worth billing for commercial-plan patients where it pays. Treat as upside. Requires NBHWC or NCHEC certification plus NPI — credentialing existing in-house team is the most direct path.
  • 0591T Initial coaching assessment
  • 0592T Individual follow-up, ≥30 min
  • 0593T Group coaching, ≥30 min
Caveats: Each payer decides whether to reimburse. Most Medicare plans currently do not. Commercial plan coverage is uneven. 5-year sunset on Category III codes — reclassification to Category I would change the picture.
IV.
Staffing the Coaching Layer
Mindspan already has the people — credentialing is the unlock
Why This Matters
The Perry Health team inside the Mindspan group already includes health coaches, dietitians, and nurses who can do prevention work. Mindspan does not need to hire externally to build a BetterBrain-style coaching layer. The question is how to deploy the existing team across billable codes — and which credentials are needed to unlock the 0591T pathway specifically.
Existing Staff Already In-Network
Nurses (RN / LPN)
The most flexible billing layer. Can work "incident to" physician services, which expands the code list considerably without additional credentialing.
  • 99490 99439 CCM time counts under clinical staff
  • 99487 99489 Complex CCM
  • 99401–99404 Preventive counseling under physician supervision
  • G0444 Depression screening
Action: No new certification needed. Highest-leverage staff for the recurring revenue base.
Existing Staff Specialty Credentialed
Registered Dietitians (RD)
Their own billable code stack independent of physician supervision. Underused asset in most clinical practices — direct revenue contributor.
  • 97802 MNT initial assessment
  • 97803 MNT follow-up, individual
  • 97804 MNT group session
  • G0270 G0271 MNT reassessment (Medicare)
Action: Confirm RDs are enrolled with payers under their own NPIs. Medicare covers MNT for diabetes and CKD automatically; commercial plans vary.
Existing Staff Credentialing Required
Health Coaches
The 0591T pathway lives here. Current coaches likely need NBHWC certification (or NCHEC) plus an NPI to bill directly. Without those, they can still contribute time to CCM under physician supervision.
  • 0591T 0592T 0593T If credentialed
  • 99490 99439 CCM under physician supervision (no certification)
  • 99411–99412 Group preventive counseling
Action: Audit existing coaches for NBHWC/NCHEC status. Sponsor certification for uncredentialed staff (~$1,500–3,000 per coach). Until then, route their time through CCM.
V.
Distribution & Pharma Partnerships
LillyDirect now has multiple Alzheimer's partners — inclusion is realistic
What's Happening
LillyDirect is Eli Lilly's direct-to-consumer digital health platform. Originally launched for Zepbound, diabetes, and migraine drugs, Lilly expanded it in March 2025 to include Alzheimer's care — specifically to drive earlier diagnosis and treatment with Kisunla (donanemab). The Alzheimer's roster has grown: Synapticure was added first, Isaac Health joined in October 2025. Lilly does not pay for referrals, but inclusion on the platform is a meaningful, branded distribution channel.
Alzheimer's · Partner 1 Live · March 2025
Synapticure on LillyDirect
First telehealth option for Alzheimer's on the platform. Care navigator within 2 days, neurologist within 2 weeks. Targets rural and underserved communities.
  • Pathway: LillyDirect → Synapticure intake → virtual neurologist → biomarker testing → infusion center referral if Kisunla indicated
  • Lilly's interest: Kisunla launched July 2024 with ~$8M Q4 2024 sales. Distribution is the bottleneck.
  • Scrutiny: Some academics and senators have raised Anti-Kickback Statute concerns; Lilly says it pays no compensation for referrals.
Alzheimer's · Partner 2 Live · October 2025
Isaac Health on LillyDirect
Joined seven months after Synapticure. 50-state coverage. Accepts major insurance, offers free initial consultation. Closed $10.5M Series A in August 2025 ($16.3M total raised) partly to support this expansion.
  • Pathway: LillyDirect → Isaac intake → virtual neurologist, psychiatrist, or cognitive specialist → diagnostic workup → ongoing care management
  • Differentiator: AI-enabled early detection, behavioral health integration, GUIDE Model participation
  • Signal: Lilly is expanding partners, not consolidating. Inclusion is realistic, not aspirational.
Migraine Channel Live · April 2026
Neura Health on LillyDirect
Added for migraine care, alongside access to Lilly's Emgality and Reyvow. Neura's broader specialty footprint also covers epilepsy, concussion, stroke recovery, and memory conditions.
  • Pathway: LillyDirect → Neura intake → telehealth neurologist within 7 days → ongoing virtual management + coaching
  • Funding: $11.4M Series A in Sept 2025 led by AHA Go Red for Women Venture Fund. $22M total raised.
Mindspan Read
The picture just shifted. With Isaac Health joining LillyDirect seven months after Synapticure, Lilly has signaled it is actively expanding the partner network, not running a single-provider exclusive. Inclusion is realistic, not aspirational.

Mindspan's pitch angle: Both current partners (Synapticure, Isaac) are virtual-only. Mindspan has brick-and-mortar clinics in MA and CA. For patients who need infusion access for Kisunla or Leqembi, a hybrid virtual + in-person model is operationally easier to coordinate than fully virtual care routing to outside infusion centers. That is a credible differentiator.

The parallel play: Eisai (Leqembi) and Biogen do not have a comparable DTC channel today. That is a wide-open lane for a clinical partner — and Mindspan's hybrid model fits an amyloid-clearing drug's care pathway directly.

The non-pharma alternative: Isaac Health also drives distribution through payer-contracted screening (ATRIO in Oregon, IBX). That model doesn't carry the Anti-Kickback Statute scrutiny pharma channels do. Worth pursuing in parallel.

Recommendation: Pursue all three in parallel — LillyDirect inclusion, Eisai partnership scoping, and MA plan screening contracts. None are mutually exclusive, and the structural advantages Mindspan holds (clinical infrastructure, in-house staff, hybrid footprint) support each one.
